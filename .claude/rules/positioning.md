# Dataships Positioning

## What Dataships Is
Dataships is an AI consent optimization platform that grows revenue for ecommerce brands. We maximize revenue from paying customers by turning them into marketing subscribers at the highest rates possible — compliantly, globally, and at scale.

We are consent infrastructure: the complete backend system that determines who a brand can market to, whether they're legally defensible, and how to optimize both continuously.

## The Three Pillars

**1. Privacy Rules Engine (Safety)**
Thousands of hours of global privacy law research encoded into a rules engine covering GDPR, CCPA, CASL, TCPA, and 19 US state laws. Makes compliance decisions in real-time, creates timestamped audit logs for every interaction, and updates proactively as laws change.

**2. Merchant Data (Context)**
Pulled from existing platforms (Shopify, Klaviyo, Attentive, Yotpo). Provides detailed context on every site visitor — consent history, suppression status, purchase behavior. Fuels the AI feedback loop and enables personalization.

**3. Network Intelligence (Improvement)**
Machine learning trained on 50M+ consent interactions across 600+ brands. Determines optimal consent interface for each shopper in real-time based on location, channel, purchase history, and context. Gets smarter with every transaction — network effects where every brand benefits from collective learnings.

## Why Infrastructure Matters

Without the infrastructure framing, Dataships risks being perceived as "a better checkbox" — a commodity feature that Shopify could build natively. The infrastructure positioning:

- **Changes the buyer**: Widgets get evaluated by junior employees. Infrastructure gets evaluated by Directors/VPs thinking about CRM strategy and long-term defensibility.
- **Changes the value conversation**: Features get compared on price. Infrastructure gets evaluated on the cost of NOT having it — regulatory risk, data quality issues, inability to scale globally.
- **Changes the competitive set**: If we're a widget, we compete with Shopify native. If we're infrastructure, we compete with the status quo of broken data foundations.
- **Changes retention**: Infrastructure becomes embedded in your data architecture. Ripping it out means rebuilding your entire consent system.
- **Unlocks the roadmap**: Infrastructure expands to progressive consent, opt-out recapture, predictive optimization. A widget is stuck optimizing checkbox language.

## What Dataships Is Not

- **Not a widget or checkout optimization tool** — The widget is the interface. The infrastructure is what makes it work.
- **Not an ESP** — We don't send emails or manage campaigns. We feed properly structured consent data to ESPs.
- **Not an SMS platform** — We collect SMS consent compliantly and feed it to SMS tools. We handle the consent layer; they handle the messaging layer.
- **Not a popup or lead capture tool** — No exit-intent popups, spin-to-win wheels, or pre-purchase lead magnets. We record consent status at the point of purchase.
- **Not identity resolution** — We do not scrape data from anonymous visitors. We believe databases should not be inflated with non-consented contacts. Focus belongs on paying customers.
- **Not manual compliance consulting** — We're not lawyers. We enforce compliance at scale, automatically.
- **Not a "nice-to-have" conversion optimization** — We improve consent rates, but we're also foundational infrastructure that determines who you can market to and whether you're legally defensible.

## Why Now

Four forces converging:

1. **Privacy regulations are compounding** — 19 US states now have comprehensive privacy laws (up from 5 in 2023). GDPR fines reach £17.5M or 4% of global revenue. Regional nuances multiplying (France requires explicit opt-in, Germany recently dropped double opt-in).
2. **First-party data is the only moat left** — Third-party cookies dying, iOS killed cross-app tracking, CAC up 60%+ since 2020. Owned audiences are the only sustainable growth lever.
3. **AI requires clean, consented data** — Every brand racing toward AI-powered personalization. Garbage in, garbage out. Consent infrastructure is the foundation for AI-driven commerce.
4. **The stakes for getting it wrong are escalating** — Regulatory fines, class action lawsuits, deliverability destruction, permanent brand damage.

## Competitive Landscape

Dataships is creating the consent optimization category. Primary alternatives:

**Shopify Native** — Free, "good enough" perception. But stuck at ~60% US opt-in and 9% UK. No AI optimization. Can inadvertently re-subscribe suppressed contacts.

**DIY Dynamic Consent** — Brands trying to build it themselves with custom Checkout Blocks. Compliance shortcomings surface quickly. Actually building a functioning consent engine requires massive resources.

**EmailGrow** — Bootstrapped copycat, 12-18 months behind. No SMS, no A/B testing, no proactive country-level rule management, suboptimal reporting.

**Why we win across all alternatives:**
- AI trained on 50M+ interactions — dataset and learning loops can't be replicated
- Network effects from 600+ brands — every customer makes the system smarter
- Real-time decisioning at scale
- Comprehensive compliance infrastructure built in
- Multi-channel coordination (email, SMS, WhatsApp)
- Proven: 2X+ MCR improvement, 2.4X CLV multiplier
