# Marketing Newsletter Guidelines

Marketing newsletters are the primary nurture channel for both prospects and customers. They build trust over time by curating relevant industry content alongside Dataships updates.

## Newsletter Format

Both prospect and customer newsletters follow a consistent three-section structure. Each issue is a curated briefing, not a product pitch.

### Structure
- Three sections per email, each self-contained with its own emoji header, body, and CTA
- Short personal intro (2-4 sentences) framing the three topics. Sign off with first name and blue heart emoji
- Each section gets an emoji icon header (robot for AI/tech, chart for data, building for compliance, email icon for email strategy, magnifying glass for deep dives)
- Arrow bullets (->) for scannable key points within sections
- One CTA button per section, placed at the bottom: "Learn More", "Run a Test", "Get the Benchmark"
- Footer: company address, unsubscribe link, manage preferences

### Subject Line Patterns
- Multi-topic format using "&" or "," separators: "Norway's Consent Shift, Smarter Opt-Outs & Shopify's AI Checkout Fees"
- Three-part structure that previews the three sections
- Mix of trend/news hooks + Dataships proof + partner value
- Sentence case, not title case. Direct but not clickbait.
- Best performers combine urgency (timely news) with utility (benchmarks, guides, checklists)

## Prospect Newsletters (Matt)

Prospect newsletters are written by Matt and position Dataships as a trusted industry curator. The goal is to build credibility by being useful before being salesy.

### Voice
- Industry curator first, vendor second. Matt filters signal from noise across ecommerce.
- Conversational but informed: "here's three things to checkout before heading into the weekend"
- Dataships content is embedded as one of three sections, never the sole focus
- Data woven into narrative: "US brands average 61% email opt-in. In the UK/EU? Just 9%."
- Partner content included as genuine value adds (guides, reports, checklists from ecosystem partners)
- Sign-off: blue heart + "Matt" with no title or company name

### Content Mix

| Section Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Industry News/Trend | Every issue (1 of 3) | Shopify updates, AI commerce, regulatory shifts. Shows Matt is on top of the industry. |
| Dataships Data/Proof | Every issue (1 of 3) | Benchmark stats, A/B test results, case study highlights. Embedded proof, not pitch. |
| Partner/Ecosystem | Most issues (1 of 3) | Guides, tools, and checklists from partners. Adds value beyond Dataships. |
| Webinar/Event | As needed | Replaces one section when promoting events. Still delivers value. |

### Prospect CTA Patterns
- Soft, value-first: "See your own numbers with a free A/B test"
- Frame as self-serve: "5-minute install, no commitment required"
- Data-as-proof CTAs: "We can show you exactly how your opt-in rates compare"
- Never hard-sell. The newsletter earns trust; the CTA offers to prove value.

## Customer Newsletters (Sue)

Customer newsletters are written by Sue and serve as the operational link between Dataships and its customer base. The voice is warmer and more product-focused than prospect newsletters.

### Voice
- Helpful CS voice: informative, clear, action-oriented
- Product updates explained with "what does this mean for you" framing
- Technical changes made accessible: "We now update their consent status directly in Klaviyo as Unsubscribed"
- More direct CTAs: "Book a check-in", "Login here", "Book time with your CSM"
- Warmer relationship tone: "Happy to answer any questions you may have"
- Sign-off: blue heart + "Sue", personal, never "The Dataships Team"

### Content Mix

| Section Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Product Update | Most issues (1-2 of 3) | New features, dashboards, Klaviyo properties, country changes. Drives adoption and awareness. |
| Industry/Compliance News | Most issues (1 of 3) | Same industry content as prospect but framed for existing users. |
| Partner Offer | Periodic | Exclusive customer offers from partners. Adds ecosystem value. |
| Event Recap | As needed | Founder Foundations recaps, webinar highlights. Shows Dataships in the community. |

### Customer CTA Patterns
- Product-oriented: "Log in to your Dataships account to explore the new experience"
- CS relationship: "Book a check-in" or "Book time with your CSM"
- Referral program: "Refer a brand to us and get $500 cash if they attend a demo"
- Partner value: exclusive offers positioned as customer-only benefits

## Content Mix & Partner Content Rules

- Partner content must genuinely serve the reader. Never include content solely because a partner asked.
- Introduce partner content in Dataships' voice, not the partner's marketing copy
- Frame partner sections as curated recommendations: "We've partnered with X to share Y"
- Partner CTAs get the same button treatment as Dataships CTAs for visual consistency
- Limit to one partner section per newsletter. Two Dataships-relevant sections minimum.
- Never let partner content overshadow Dataships data or product updates in the same issue

## Prospect vs. Customer: Key Differences

| Prospect | Customer |
|---|---|
| Industry curator tone, Matt's voice | CS partner tone, Sue's voice |
| Dataships proof embedded subtly | Product updates front and center |
| Soft CTAs (A/B test, benchmarks) | Direct CTAs (book CSM, log in) |
| Build credibility before selling | Drive adoption and expansion |
| Both: 3-section format, emoji headers, arrow bullets, personal sign-off | |
