# Compliance & Regulatory Content Guidelines

When writing about compliance and privacy regulations, we walk a careful line: make it accessible without dumbing it down, and always connect back to business impact.

## Voice

- Approachable expert: we translate legalese into plain English
- Can use slightly more casual touches to lighten inherently dry content
- Always quantify the stakes: "$500 to $10,000 per message in damages"
- Use FAQ format for regulatory explainers: it's the most scannable format
- Never give legal advice. Use "we recommend" or "brands should consider" not "you must"
- Always end by connecting compliance requirements to Dataships' automatic handling

## Framing

The Dataships approach to compliance content:

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| A growth opportunity: compliance done right = more subscribers | A burden: "you have to comply or face fines" |
| Complexity that Dataships handles automatically | Complexity that should scare you into buying |
| Evolving landscape that requires infrastructure | Static rules you can set and forget |
| Global nuance that requires real-time decisioning | Simple checkboxes you can copy-paste |
