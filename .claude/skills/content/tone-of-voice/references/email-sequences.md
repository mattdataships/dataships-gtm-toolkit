# Email Sequence Guidelines

Email sequences are our highest-performing outreach channel. Two distinct sub-voices: event-driven and cold outbound. Both share the core Dataships voice but deploy it differently.

## Event Sequences (Dinner Invites + Post-Event Follow-Ups)

Event sequences are our top performers. They drive replies because they feel personal, exclusive, and low-pressure.

### Dinner Invite Tone
- Casual and personal: like a text from a friend who's hosting
- Name the restaurant, the date, the city: "ecom dinner invite - thurs 7pm @ sirrah"
- Short. The entire email is 3-5 sentences
- No pitch in the invite itself: the dinner IS the value
- Sign off as a human: "Matt" or "Matthew," never "The Dataships Team"
- RSVP mechanism is simple: "Reply if you'd like me to sort your pass"

### Post-Event Follow-Up Tone
- Reference the specific event: "Thanks for coming out to the Chotto Matte dinner"
- Acknowledge if you didn't connect directly: "I'm not sure you had the opportunity to chat live with anyone at Dataships"
- Transition naturally to value: "wanted to send through some info in case audience growth/compliance are priorities"
- Use casual data points: "We get 3-5X higher sign up rates for email and ~30X for SMS"
- Close with low-pressure offer: "Happy to get more info over to you/whoever handles retention on your team"
- Follow-up bumps are 2-3 sentences max: "Just bumping here in case this got buried post-event"

## Cold Outbound Sequences

Cold outbound is more structured but still personal. Two proven approaches:

### Approach A: Personalized Metric Hook
- Subject line uses their actual data: "{Name} - {Brand} is at X% opt-in (should be Y%)"
- Open with a relevant case study from their vertical/scale
- Highlight specific metrics that create urgency: "+76.48% email subscriber growth"
- Close with a soft ask: "Happy to get you set up or loop in whoever handles retention"
- PS for checkout audit: "Took a quick look at your checkout - looks like the default pre-ticked setup."

### Approach B: Free A/B Test Series
- Lead with momentum: "A lot has happened at Dataships since we last spoke"
- Show visual proof: include A/B test screenshots in the email
- Step 2: Send relevant case studies as follow-up value
- Step 3: Share the benchmark report as a resource
- Tone is warmer, more relationship-focused: "Hopefully chat soon"
- Each step adds value rather than just asking for time

## Universal Sequence Rules

- Subject lines: lowercase, personal, specific. Never title case. Never clickbait.
- First name only in greeting: "Hey Leah," never "Dear Ms. Smith"
- Sign off as the individual: "Matt" or "Mark" or "Michael" with title in signature
- Keep body under 150 words per step. Shorter = more replies.
- One CTA per email. Don't give them three things to click.
- Follow-up bumps acknowledge the previous email: "Just bumping in case this got buried"
- Never use "I hope this email finds you well" or any generic opener
- Personalize with their brand name, checkout details, or vertical-relevant case study
- The free A/B test is our most powerful CTA: use it as the primary ask
- Mention ease of setup: "5 minutes" and "plug and play" reduce friction
