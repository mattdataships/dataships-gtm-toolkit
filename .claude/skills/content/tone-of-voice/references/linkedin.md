# LinkedIn Post Guidelines

LinkedIn is where Matt and the Dataships team build personal authority and drive top-of-funnel awareness. Posts should feel like insider dispatches from someone deep in the ecom/consent space.

## Personal (Matt / Team Member) Posts

### Voice
- First person, conversational. Like sharing a learning with a smart peer over coffee.
- Mix of tactical insights, hot takes on industry trends, and behind-the-scenes from events
- Data-backed opinions: "We analyzed X and found Y" is stronger than "I think Y"
- Willingness to challenge conventional wisdom: question popup culture, default consent, compliance theater

### Structure
- Hook line (first 1-2 lines visible before "see more"): a stat, a provocative claim, or a contrarian take
- Build the argument in 3-5 short paragraphs
- Use line breaks liberally: LinkedIn rewards scannable formatting
- End with a question, a CTA, or a one-line takeaway
- No hashtag spam. 2-3 relevant hashtags max, placed at the end

## Company Page Posts

### Voice
- Slightly more polished than personal posts, but still direct and metric-forward
- Mix of: case study highlights, product updates, event recaps, benchmark data drops, hiring
- Lead with the result or insight, not the announcement
- Use customer quotes as the hook when possible

### Content Mix

| Post Type | Frequency | Lead With |
|---|---|---|
| Case Study Highlight | 2-3x/month | The metric: "$363K in repeat revenue" or "7X opt-in lift" |
| Product Update | 1-2x/month | What it enables: "Now you can..." not "We just launched..." |
| Event Recap | As needed | A photo or takeaway, not an event announcement |
| Benchmark Data Drop | 1x/month | The most surprising data point from our network |
| Team/Culture | 1-2x/month | Something real: event photos, team wins, hiring |
