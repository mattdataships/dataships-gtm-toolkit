# Case Study Guidelines

Case studies are our most powerful sales asset. They answer one question: "Will this actually make me money, and can I prove it?"

## Headline Formula

Every case study headline follows one of three proven patterns:

### Pattern 1: Revenue/Outcome Headline
"How [Brand] Drove [Dollar Amount] in [Outcome] with Dataships"
- How Ekster Drove $363K in Repeat Purchase Revenue with Dataships
- How Denver Modern Drove $2M in Repeat Purchases with Dataships

### Pattern 2: Metric Lift Headline
"How [Brand] [Multiplier/Percentage] [Metric] with Dataships"
- How Fusion 7X'd Email Opt-In Rate with Dataships
- How eBOOST Tripled Email Opt-In Rates with Dataships

### Pattern 3: Transformation Headline
"How [Brand] [Transformation Verb] [Business Outcome]"
- How Loop Earplugs Turned Global Compliance into a Growth Engine
- How IQBAR Transformed Checkout into a Repeat Purchase Powerhouse

## Structure

Every case study follows this arc:

| Section | Purpose | Guidelines |
|---|---|---|
| **Hero Stats** | Immediate proof of value | 3-4 key metrics displayed prominently. Each stat has two lines: the metric (4 characters or less, e.g. "85%", "$2M", "491%") and a sub-line (27 characters or less, e.g. "Email MCR, up from 39%"). |
| **Brand Context** | Establish credibility | 1-2 sentences: vertical, scale, what they sell. Name the brand, their platform (Shopify Plus), and their ESP. |
| **Challenge** | Create recognition | What problem were they solving? Frame as a growth opportunity, not a compliance headache. Describe the customer's prior setup gracefully — highlight the growth gap without making the brand sound irresponsible or negligent. Words like "liability," "broken," or "failing" should never describe a customer's own systems. Use their words via direct quote where possible. |
| **Solution** | Show how it works | Brief, mechanical: what we did, how long setup took, what the A/B test showed. Under 150 words. |
| **Results** | The payoff | Specific numbers with context. Revenue attributed, subscriber growth, CLV impact. Tie back to business outcomes. |
| **Quote** | Human endorsement | Direct quote from their team (Director+). Should reinforce business impact, not just "great tool." |
| **CTA** | Drive action | "Want to see what Dataships could do for [your vertical]? Start with a free A/B test." |

## Tone & Style Rules

- Write in third person past tense: "Ekster implemented Dataships and saw..."
- Front-load the result: the reader should see the outcome in the first 10 seconds
- Use the brand's actual vertical terminology ("functional nutrition" not "food brand")
- Every case study must include at least one pull quote — a styled, visually distinct block quote from the customer (Director+ level). Pull quotes reinforce credibility and break up the page. If a full interview isn't available, use any direct quote sourced from calls, emails, or written feedback
- Avoid superlatives without proof: "best" needs a benchmark, "fastest" needs a timeline
- Connect opt-in improvements to downstream revenue: never leave the story at "higher opt-ins"
- Reference the A/B test as proof methodology: we show our work
- Keep the total word count between 600-900 words. These are results briefs, not whitepapers
- When referring to Dataships, use the approved terminology from the positioning guide: "consent optimization platform" or "consent infrastructure." Never invent alternative descriptors like "dynamic consent platform," "consent engine," or "consent tool"
- Enforce the em dash limit strictly in case studies. Maximum 2-3 per piece. Case studies are particularly prone to em dash overuse because of the before/after and parenthetical data patterns. Rewrite using commas, colons, periods, or parentheses instead
