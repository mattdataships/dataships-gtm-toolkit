# Website Copy Guidelines

Website copy is our most visible content. It needs to convert visitors into demo bookings and A/B test signups in under 30 seconds of scanning.

## Website Voice

Website copy is the most distilled version of the Dataships voice: punchy, metric-forward, and structured for scanning. Every sentence earns its place.

**Key principles:**
- Headlines state the outcome, not the feature: "Grow your revenue" not "Optimize your consent"
- Subheadlines add the mechanism: "AI consent optimization platform" explains how
- Every section answers: "What's in it for me?" within the first line
- Use the consent-as-infrastructure framing: "Consent is a revenue problem. We built the platform to solve it."
- Proof points appear early and often: "600+ brands," "50M+ interactions," "25X ROI"

## Page-by-Page Patterns

### Homepage
- Hero: Outcome headline + mechanism subheadline + two CTAs (primary: Book a Demo, secondary: A/B Test)
- Logo bar: "High value marketing lists for 600+ brands"
- Problem framing: "Most brands lose half their paying customers to broken consent collection"
- Infrastructure framing: "Consent is a revenue problem. We built the platform to solve it."
- Stats section: Use real averages (new contacts/mo, new LTV/mo, ROI)
- Feature sections: Each gets a benefit headline + 1 sentence explanation + proof metric
- Social proof: Customer quotes from named individuals with title and company
- Bottom CTA: A/B test focus with specific language about what they'll learn

### Product Pages (Bigger Audiences, Compliance, Performance)
- Each page owns one pillar of the value prop
- Open with a clear problem statement, not a feature list
- Use before/after stat blocks: "39% -> 81%" with visual emphasis
- Include vertical-specific examples when possible
- Feature descriptions are 1-2 sentences max with a benefit-first structure
- End each section with relevant customer testimonial

### Integration Pages
- Follow a consistent template: integration headline, 1-paragraph value prop, how it works (4 steps), CTA
- Lead with what the integration enables, not what it connects
- "Syncs properly consented subscribers" not "Connects Dataships to Klaviyo"
- Include trust signals: "Trusted by hundreds of [Platform] users including [Brand, Brand, Brand]"
- How-it-works steps are 1 sentence each, starting with a verb

### About Page
- Origin story is brief and purposeful: not autobiographical
- Lead with mission: "Building consent infrastructure for ecommerce"
- Frame the founding story around the insight, not the founders' resumes
- Stats bar: Founded, People, HQ, Funding, Brands: short and scannable
- Tone is slightly warmer than product pages but still direct

## CTA Patterns

| CTA Type | Primary Text | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Book a demo | Default CTA throughout site |
| **Secondary** | Schedule your A/B test -> | Bottom of pages, post-content |
| **Tertiary** | See your actual numbers | Supporting CTA with specificity |
| **Content** | Read the benchmark / See the case study | Supporting content links |
