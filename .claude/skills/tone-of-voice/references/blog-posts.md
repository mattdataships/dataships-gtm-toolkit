# Blog Post Guidelines

Blog posts serve two functions: drive organic traffic around consent/retention topics, and position Dataships as the authority in the consent optimization category.

## Blog Voice

The blog voice is consultative and advisory. We're the smart friend who works in ecom and is sharing what they've learned. Slightly more educational than sales content, but never academic. Always actionable.

**Key characteristics:**
- Conversational authority: explain complex topics (compliance, AI, retention) without talking down to the reader
- Myth-busting mentality: challenge assumptions with data. "Marketers have long believed X. But data shows Y."
- Second-person address throughout: "you," "your brand," "your checkout"
- Problem -> evidence -> solution -> action arc in every post
- Case studies woven in as proof points, not appendices
- Industry references for credibility: cite Klaviyo benchmarks, reference well-known brands' strategies

## Structure

### Opening (Hook)
Every post opens with one of these patterns:
- Surprising stat: "70% of Shopify stores still rely on incentives to drive first-time purchases"
- Myth challenge: "Marketers have long believed that mature shoppers aren't receptive to SMS. Data says otherwise."
- Pain recognition: "Every completed purchase is a win, but what happens next matters even more."
- Trend framing: "Retention marketing has become one of the highest-leverage growth opportunities for DTC brands."

### Body
- Use clear H2/H3 subheadings that act as signposts: a reader skimming should get the argument from headers alone
- Break content into scannable sections: short paragraphs (2-4 sentences), bullet lists, bold callouts
- Embed data naturally in narrative: "SMS opt-in rates hover around 0.5%" reads better than a standalone stat
- Include at least one worked example or calculation (e.g., revenue math showing what's left on the table)
- Reference 2-3 real brands per post as proof points
- Use strategic questions to prompt self-reflection: "Are enough of the right customers opting in?"

### Closing
- Transition from insight to action: "Ready to see for yourself?"
- Offer a specific CTA: free A/B test, demo, audit guide, benchmark report
- Keep it to 2-3 sentences max: don't re-summarize the post
- End with confidence, not desperation: "Run a free A/B test" not "Please contact us"

## Blog Categories & Angle

| Category | Angle | Tone Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Thought Leadership** | Category creation: why consent is infrastructure, not a feature | Most authoritative. Big-picture. References industry trends and competitive dynamics. |
| **Tactical/How-To** | Actionable guides: audits, A/B testing, flow optimization | Most instructional. Step-by-step. Includes formulas, benchmarks, worked examples. |
| **Product Updates** | New features, integrations, quarterly retros | Most enthusiastic. Celebrate milestones. Show momentum. Connect features to outcomes. |
| **Compliance/Regulatory** | Law breakdowns (Texas SB140, GDPR updates) | Most accessible. Make complex legal topics digestible. Use FAQs. Connect to business impact. |
| **Benchmarks/Data** | Industry data and trends from our network | Most data-dense. Lead with numbers. Position Dataships data as authoritative source. |
