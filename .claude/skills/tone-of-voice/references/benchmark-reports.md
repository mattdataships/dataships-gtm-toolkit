# Benchmark Report Guidelines

The Consent Benchmark Report is our flagship thought leadership asset. It establishes Dataships as the authoritative source on consent data. Future benchmark reports and data-heavy content should follow these patterns.

## Report Voice

More authoritative and measured than other content. This is the one place where we lean into our data advantage and speak with category-defining confidence. Think analyst report meets operator playbook.

**Key characteristics:**
- Lead with proprietary data: "Analysis of 50M+ consent interactions across 600+ brands"
- Use definitive framing: "The definitive guide to checkout consent performance"
- Present regional breakdowns: show the complexity of the global landscape
- Pair every data point with an actionable insight: don't just show the number, tell them what to do with it
- Use comparative framing: default Shopify vs. optimized consent, US vs. EU vs. UK
- Position Dataships data as the industry standard: "According to our network of 600+ brands..."

## Data Visualization Principles

- Before/after format for maximum impact: "US email opt-in: 61% default -> 93% optimized"
- Use tables for regional comparisons: show the complexity visually
- Bold the key number in every data point
- Include methodology notes for credibility: sample size, time period, inclusion criteria
- Every chart or table should have a one-line insight caption that tells the reader what to think

## Structure Template

1. Executive summary with key findings (3-5 bullets)
2. Methodology and data sourcing
3. Global overview with aggregate metrics
4. Regional deep-dives (US, UK, EU, AU, CA) with country-specific insights
5. Channel breakdown (email vs. SMS) with separate benchmarks
6. Optimization opportunities: where brands leave the most on the table
7. Actionable recommendations with specific next steps
8. CTA: Free A/B test to benchmark your own performance against these numbers
